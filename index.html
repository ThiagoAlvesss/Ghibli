Totoro

Totoro is a beloved character from the Studio Ghibli film "My Neighbor Totoro." It is a large, friendly forest spirit that helps two young sisters, Satsuki and Mei, during their adventures in the countryside. Totoro is known for its gentle nature, magical abilities, and iconic appearance, including its large ears and fluffy body.

Kaonachi

Kaonashi, also known as "No-Face," is a character from the Studio Ghibli film "Spirited Away." It is a spirit that can absorb the emotions and characteristics of those around it, leading to its transformation into a more monstrous form. Kaonashi is often seen as a symbol of loneliness and the desire for connection.

Susuwatari

Susuwatari, also known as "soot sprites," are small, black, dust-like creatures that appear in various Studio Ghibli films, particularly in "My Neighbor Totoro" and "Spirited Away." They are depicted as playful and mischievous beings that inhabit dusty corners of houses and other places. Susuwatari are often seen helping to clean up dust and dirt, but they can also cause mischief if disturbed.
